# The Neon Lillaby

The city sleeps in shades of neon blue,
Beneath the hum of wires and tired rain.
The streets are silver where the day was new,
And shadows wash away the grease and stain.

The rhythmic pulse of traffic starts to fade,
A distant siren sings a lonely song.
In every corner where the light is delayed,
The midnight secrets move their way along.

The high-rise windows glow like embers dim,
A thousand lives behind a pane of glass.
Each one a story filled up to the brim,
That watches all the quiet hours pass.

The weary traveler finds a place to rest,
While night-shift souls begin their steady grind.
The moon is leaning low toward the west,
Leaving the busy morning far behind.

The park is still, the benches cold and bare,
The fountain whispers to the empty air.
A scent of jasmine lingers in the flare,
Of streetlamps casting circles here and there.

No ticking clocks can rush the velvet dark,
It holds the world in soft and steady hands.
From harbor lights to every silent park,
A peaceful spell is cast across the lands.

Then comes the silver gray of early light,
To brush the edges of the sleeping towers.
The stars surrender to the ending night,
As dew begins to wet the garden flowers.

The world awakes with coffee and with lead,
The stillness breaks as metal meets the stone.
The magic of the midnight hour is fled,
And once again, the day resumes its throne.
